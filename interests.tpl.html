Kent

Kent is a city in the U.S. state of Ohio and the largest city in Portage County which is located along Cuyahoga River in Northeast Ohio. It was founded in 1805 and was known for many years as Franklin Mills. Now it has the population of approximately 30,000 residents.

Kent State University

Kent State University’s main campus is large (1,347 acres or 545 ha) and spacious, with state-of-the-art facilities and a park-like setting with walkways and trees. Kent State has been awarded the Tree Campus USA designation for several years in a row, in recognition of its commitment to campus forestry management and environmental stewardship
